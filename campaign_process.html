Ad Campaign: Healthy Eating

Ad campaign centered around convincing young adults to adopt healthier eating behaviors; particularly in regards to the cost of healthy food.

I aimed to tie the routine based relationship that many young people have with cosemetic products to their relationship with food. The text was treated as a graphic element to give the piece more visual interest. Borrowing iconography from farming and healthy food’s packaging also helps to give the pieces a warmer and less clinical feel.

One essential part of the process for this project was writing goals. I used an "OIIC" format to outline my goals. OIIC is an acronym for "objective, issue, insight, and challenge." Here's an example:

Objective: Convince young adults (18-30) that buying locally grown/sold produce is worth the cost because of the health benefits.
Issue: Young adults are aware that eating healthy foods is good for your health. However, they still do not make it a high priority.
Insight: Young adults are more concerned with their appearance than both younger and older age demographics. They spend a lot of money on products that they feel make themselves appear more attractive.
Challenge: Highlight the positive effect that eating healthy foods has on one’s appearance.

This was key in helping me find my exact target audience, and in coming up with a game plan for how best to communicate to that audience.

Because of the simplicity of the design that I landed on, good photography was essential. I didn't have access to a lighting studio, so it took some time and ingenuity to get it right. Eventually by using some drawing paper for a seamless white background and some very precarious lighting techniques, I was able to get some decent shots. Following this, I worked through many different graphic and typogrphic ideas, finally settling on a sort of faux farm sticker design. I wanted it to look like a label that you might see in a grocery store or at a farmers market, so I leaned into the fake distressed aesthetic that many healthy and organic brands capitalize on. Finally, after many hours in photoshop making up for my sub-par product photography, I finalized my design.
